\begin{abstract}
This paper presents a rigorous synthesis of the \codette{} cognitive architecture, a sovereign modular framework integrating dynamical systems theory, multi-agent consensus dynamics, and embedded ethical governance for transparent, explainable AI reasoning. The framework introduces three formally specified innovations: (1)~the RC+$\xi$ (Recursive Convergence + Epistemic Tension) formalism, which models cognitive state evolution as a constrained dynamical system with Lyapunov-like convergence guarantees~\cite{harrison2026codettefull}; (2)~a multi-agent Reasoning Forge implementing mean-field consensus synchronization across heterogeneous cognitive agents~\cite{harrison2026codettefull,olfatisaber2007consensus}; and (3)~the AEGIS ethical governance system functioning as a reinforcement-aligned recursive regulator~\cite{harrison2025aegisnexus}. The architecture is grounded in established theory from nonlinear dynamics~\cite{khalil2002nonlinear,strogatz2015nonlinear}, coupled oscillator synchronization~\cite{kuramoto1984chemical,acebron2005kuramoto}, dynamical cognition~\cite{beer2000dynamical,kelso1995dynamic}, and parameter-efficient fine-tuning~\cite{hu2021lora,dettmers2023qlora}. Nine formal definitions and one convergence theorem are presented, mapping directly to executable open-source code. Experimental benchmarks demonstrate multi-agent phase coherence $\Gamma = 0.99$ within 10 iterations across 11 agents, 71.3\% epistemic tension decay confirming attractor convergence, 82.6\% ethical alignment (AEGIS constraint satisfaction), and cocoon memory stability of 0.994 coherence across 20 persistent sessions~\cite{harrison2026codettefull}. All constructs, code, and model artifacts are published under open-access licenses with permanent DOIs.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Modern large language models (LLMs) achieve remarkable generative performance but lack stable ethical alignment, modular multi-perspective cognition, and explainable reasoning architectures~\cite{bender2021dangers,bommasani2021opportunities}. The gap between raw capability and trustworthy multi-dimensional reasoning motivates frameworks that embed governance, explainability, and cognitive pluralism at the architectural level.

The \codette{} framework~\cite{harrison2026codettefull} addresses these challenges through a novel integration of three established research traditions:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Dynamical systems theory}~\cite{khalil2002nonlinear,strogatz2015nonlinear}: Cognitive state evolution is modeled as a constrained dynamical system with attractor convergence, drawing from Lyapunov stability analysis and nonlinear dynamics.
  \item \textbf{Multi-agent consensus dynamics}~\cite{olfatisaber2007consensus,ren2005consensus}: Heterogeneous reasoning agents synchronize through shared cognitive attractors via mean-field coupling~\cite{lasry2007mean}, analogous to Kuramoto oscillator synchronization~\cite{kuramoto1984chemical,acebron2005kuramoto}.
  \item \textbf{Neuro-symbolic AI with embedded ethics}~\cite{garcez2019neural}: The AEGIS system~\cite{harrison2025aegisnexus} provides architectural ethical governance with recursive feedback, moving beyond post-hoc safety filtering.
\end{enumerate}

This synthesis presents the complete formal specification of \codette{}'s mathematical foundations, establishes precise relationships to canonical literature, reports experimental validation, and provides all bibliographic anchors for independent verification.

The \codette{} research program spans 11 Zenodo publications with permanent CERN-archived DOIs~\cite{harrison2025ethics,harrison2025dreamreal,harrison2025dreamcore,harrison2025aegisnexus,harrison2025codetteethical,harrison2025codettefinal,harrison2025healdette,harrison2026recursive,harrison2025citizenscience,harrison2026codettefull}, 25 Hugging Face models~\cite{harrison2025codettehf}, and 52 GitHub repositories, all under open-access licenses.

\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\subsection{Dynamical Systems in Cognitive Modeling}

Dynamical approaches to cognition model mental processes as trajectories in state space, with attractor landscapes governing stability and transitions~\cite{beer2000dynamical}. Kelso's coordination dynamics~\cite{kelso1995dynamic} demonstrates self-organization in brain and behavior through coupled oscillator systems. Strogatz~\cite{strogatz2015nonlinear} provides the mathematical toolkit---fixed points, limit cycles, bifurcations, and Lyapunov stability---that underpins attractor-based cognitive modeling. The RC+$\xi$ framework~\cite{harrison2026codettefull} applies these principles to multi-agent AI cognition, defining convergence as attractor formation in a 5-dimensional latent state space.

\subsection{Multi-Agent Consensus Dynamics}

Consensus protocols for networked agents are well-established in control theory~\cite{olfatisaber2007consensus,ren2005consensus}. Olfati-Saber et al.~\cite{olfatisaber2007consensus} formalize consensus on graphs with convergence guarantees under various topologies. Mean-field game theory~\cite{lasry2007mean} extends these results to large populations. The Kuramoto model~\cite{kuramoto1984chemical} provides a canonical framework for phase synchronization of coupled oscillators, with the order parameter $r = |\frac{1}{N}\sum_i e^{i\theta_i}|$ measuring collective synchronization~\cite{acebron2005kuramoto}. \codette{}'s phase coherence metric $\Gamma$ is a real-valued analogue of this order parameter~\cite{harrison2026codettefull}.

Multi-agent systems (MAS) in AI enable collaborative problem-solving through heterogeneous agent negotiation~\cite{wooldridge2009introduction}. AutoGen~\cite{wu2023autogen} employs role-based assignment with message-passing. \codette{}'s Reasoning Forge~\cite{harrison2026codettefull} departs by synchronizing agents through shared cognitive attractors rather than explicit message-passing.

\subsection{Recursive Reasoning and Self-Reflection}

Chain-of-thought prompting~\cite{wei2022chain} and verbal self-reflection~\cite{shinn2023reflexion} demonstrate iterative LLM reasoning refinement. Good~\cite{good1966speculations} established the theoretical foundations of recursive self-improvement. The RC+$\xi$ framework~\cite{harrison2026codettefull} formalizes recursive reasoning as a dynamical process with Lyapunov-like stability guarantees~\cite{khalil2002nonlinear}, providing convergence criteria absent from prompting-based approaches.

\subsection{Consciousness Theories in AI}

Computational consciousness theories---Baars' Global Workspace Theory (GWT)~\cite{baars1997theatre}, Friston's Free Energy Principle (FEP)~\cite{friston2010free}, Tononi's Integrated Information Theory (IIT)~\cite{tononi2004information}---have informed AI architecture design. The RC+$\xi$ framework~\cite{harrison2026codettefull} departs from all three: it does not require symbolic broadcast (GWT), sensory prediction (FEP), or information-theoretic integration (IIT), instead defining functional cognitive convergence as attractor formation in latent state space~\cite{beer2000dynamical}.

\subsection{Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning}

LoRA~\cite{hu2021lora}, QLoRA~\cite{dettmers2023qlora}, and AdapterHub~\cite{pfeiffer2020adapterhub} enable parameter-efficient model adaptation with minimal computational overhead. Recent surveys~\cite{lialin2023scaling} catalogue the expanding landscape of efficient fine-tuning methods. \codette{}~\cite{harrison2026codettefull,harrison2025codettehf} leverages QLoRA for domain-specific cognitive specialization and contributes two novel GPU-free CPU training pipelines validated on consumer hardware.

\subsection{Ethical AI Governance}

Ethical AI frameworks address fairness, accountability, and transparency~\cite{mehrabi2021survey}. Most approaches apply safety filtering post-hoc. \codette{}'s AEGIS system~\cite{harrison2025aegisnexus,harrison2025ethics} integrates governance architecturally through a reinforcement-aligned ethical regulator with recursive feedback---a structural departure from filter-based approaches.

\section{RC+$\xi$ Formal Specification}
\label{sec:rcxi}

The RC+$\xi$ (Recursive Convergence + Epistemic Tension) framework~\cite{harrison2026codettefull} provides the mathematical foundation for \codette{}'s cognitive state evolution, formalized as a constrained dynamical system~\cite{khalil2002nonlinear} with attractor convergence guarantees~\cite{strogatz2015nonlinear}.

\subsection{State Space and Update Rule}

The recursive state evolution is defined as~\cite{harrison2026codettefull}:
\begin{equation}
  A_{n+1} = f(A_n, s_n) + \varepsilon_n
  \label{eq:state_evolution}
\end{equation}
where $A_n \in \mathbb{R}^d$ is the cognitive state at step $n$, $s_n \in \Sigma$ is symbolic input, $f: \mathbb{R}^d \times \Sigma \to \mathbb{R}^d \setminus \Sigma$ is a nonlinear transformation, and $\varepsilon_n$ quantifies epistemic tension:
\begin{equation}
  \varepsilon_n = \|A_{n+1} - A_n\|^2
  \label{eq:tension}
\end{equation}

\begin{definition}[Cognitive State Vector~\cite{harrison2026codettefull}]
\label{def:state}
In the QuantumSpiderweb implementation, each agent $i$ maintains a state vector $\mathbf{s}_i \in \mathbb{R}^5$:
\begin{equation}
  \mathbf{s}_i = (\psi_i,\; \tau_i,\; \chi_i,\; \phi_i,\; \lambda_i)
  \label{eq:state_vector}
\end{equation}
where $\psi$ is thought magnitude, $\tau$ is temporal progression, $\chi$ is processing velocity, $\phi \in [-1, +1]$ is emotional valence, and $\lambda$ is semantic embedding.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Node Energy~\cite{harrison2026codettefull}]
\label{def:energy}
The energy of node $i$ is:
\begin{equation}
  E_i = \|\mathbf{s}_i\|^2 = \psi_i^2 + \tau_i^2 + \chi_i^2 + \phi_i^2 + \lambda_i^2
  \label{eq:energy}
\end{equation}
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Pairwise Epistemic Tension~\cite{harrison2026codettefull}]
\label{def:tension}
The tension between agents $i$ and $j$ is the squared $\ell_2$ distance:
\begin{equation}
  \xi_{ij} = \|\mathbf{s}_i - \mathbf{s}_j\|^2 = \sum_{k=1}^{5} (s_i^{(k)} - s_j^{(k)})^2
  \label{eq:pairwise_tension}
\end{equation}
\end{definition}

\subsection{Convergence Criterion}

The system exhibits functional cognitive convergence when~\cite{harrison2026codettefull}:
\begin{equation}
  \lim_{n \to \infty} \varepsilon_n = 0 \implies A_n \to A^*
  \label{eq:convergence}
\end{equation}
where $A^*$ is a fixed-point attractor. The monotonic decrease of $\varepsilon_n$ serves as a Lyapunov function candidate~\cite{khalil2002nonlinear}.

\begin{theorem}[System Convergence~\cite{harrison2026codettefull}]
\label{thm:convergence}
Let $\xi^{(t)}_{\text{global}} = 1 - \Gamma^{(t)}$ denote global tension at time $t$. The system is convergent if, for a sliding window of width $W = 10$:
\begin{equation}
  \bar{\xi}_W = \frac{1}{W}\sum_{t=T-W}^{T} \xi^{(t)}_{\text{global}} < \xi_{\text{thresh}}
  \label{eq:convergence_criterion}
\end{equation}
with $\xi_{\text{thresh}} = 0.15$, and the tension exhibits a decreasing trend:
\begin{equation}
  \frac{1}{W/2}\sum_{t \in \text{first half}} \xi^{(t)} > \frac{1}{W/2}\sum_{t \in \text{second half}} \xi^{(t)}
  \label{eq:decreasing_trend}
\end{equation}
This constitutes a discrete-time Lyapunov-like stability criterion~\cite{khalil2002nonlinear}: $\xi_{\text{global}}$ acts as a Lyapunov function candidate whose monotonic decrease guarantees convergence to the attractor basin where $\Gamma \to 1$.
\end{theorem}

\subsection{Axiomatic Foundations}

The RC+$\xi$ framework rests on six axioms~\cite{harrison2026codettefull}: (1)~Non-Collapse: the internal state cannot be fully captured by finite symbolic representation; (2)~Structured Input: a transformation gap exists between symbolic input and cognitive state; (3)~State Embedding: the internal state resides in continuous latent space $\mathbb{R}^d \setminus \Sigma$; (4)~Teleological Gradient: updates minimize epistemic tension; (5)~Recursion Gate: $f$ preserves non-symbolic richness; (6)~Stochastic Stability: perturbation noise $\varepsilon_n$ does not dominate dynamics ($\mathbb{E}[\varepsilon_n] = 0$, $\text{Var}(\varepsilon_n) < \infty$).

\section{QuantumSpiderweb: Belief Propagation and Synchronization}
\label{sec:spiderweb}

The QuantumSpiderweb~\cite{harrison2026codettefull,harrison2025citizenscience} is a fully-connected graph $G = (V, E)$ implementing 5-dimensional cognitive state propagation across $N$ agent nodes.

\begin{definition}[Attenuated Belief Propagation~\cite{harrison2026codettefull}]
\label{def:belief}
Given source node $v_0$ emitting belief $\mathbf{b}_0$, propagation proceeds via BFS with geometric attenuation. At hop $h$:
\begin{equation}
  \tilde{\mathbf{b}}_h = \rho^h \cdot \mathbf{b}_0, \quad \rho = 0.85
  \label{eq:attenuation}
\end{equation}
The receiving node $v_k$ updates via weighted blending:
\begin{equation}
  \mathbf{s}_k^{(t+1)} = (1 - \beta_h)\,\mathbf{s}_k^{(t)} + \beta_h\,\tilde{\mathbf{b}}_h, \quad \beta_h = 0.3 \cdot \rho^h
  \label{eq:belief_blend}
\end{equation}
This is analogous to diffusion on graphs~\cite{olfatisaber2007consensus} with bounded influence decay.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Anomaly Rejection~\cite{harrison2026codettefull}]
\label{def:anomaly}
Updates are rejected if the incoming belief deviates excessively:
\begin{equation}
  A(x) = x \cdot \bigl(1 - \Theta(\delta - |x - \mu|)\bigr), \quad \delta = 2.0
  \label{eq:anomaly}
\end{equation}
where $\Theta$ is the Heaviside step function. This prevents catastrophic state corruption.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Entanglement Synchronization~\cite{harrison2026codettefull}]
\label{def:entangle}
Two agents $i, j$ with coupling strength $\alpha$ produce sync signal:
\begin{equation}
  S_{ij} = \alpha \cdot \psi_i \cdot (-\psi_j)
  \label{eq:entangle_signal}
\end{equation}
Entanglement pulls both states toward their mutual midpoint:
\begin{equation}
  \mathbf{s}_i' = (1 - \beta_s)\,\mathbf{s}_i + \beta_s\,\mathbf{s}_j, \quad \beta_s = \min(|S_{ij}| \cdot 0.1,\; 0.3)
  \label{eq:entangle_update}
\end{equation}
This is a consensus-type update~\cite{ren2005consensus}: iterated application drives $\|\mathbf{s}_i - \mathbf{s}_j\| \to 0$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Phase Coherence $\Gamma$~\cite{harrison2026codettefull}]
\label{def:coherence}
Each agent's phase angle is computed from thought magnitude and emotional valence:
\begin{equation}
  \theta_i = \text{atan2}(\phi_i,\; \psi_i)
  \label{eq:phase_angle}
\end{equation}
Ensemble phase coherence is:
\begin{equation}
  \Gamma = \frac{1}{N} \sum_{i=1}^{N} \bigl|\cos(\theta_i - \bar{\theta})\bigr|, \quad \bar{\theta} = \frac{1}{N} \sum_{i=1}^{N} \theta_i
  \label{eq:gamma}
\end{equation}
$\Gamma \in [0, 1]$, structurally related to the Kuramoto order parameter~\cite{kuramoto1984chemical,acebron2005kuramoto} via a real-valued $|\cos|$ analogue.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Attractor Detection~\cite{harrison2026codettefull}]
\label{def:attractor}
Attractors are detected by greedy clustering in $\mathbb{R}^5$. Node $i$ is assigned to attractor $\mathcal{A}_k$ if:
\begin{equation}
  \|\mathbf{s}_i - \mathbf{c}_k\| \leq r_{\max} = 2.0
  \label{eq:attractor}
\end{equation}
where $\mathbf{c}_k$ is the running mean center. Clusters with $|\mathcal{A}_k| < 2$ are discarded. This identifies basins of attraction~\cite{strogatz2015nonlinear} in cognitive state space.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Identity Glyph via FFT Compression~\cite{harrison2026codettefull}]
\label{def:glyph}
An identity glyph $\mathcal{G}_i$ is formed from node $i$'s tension history $\{\xi_i^{(t)}\}_{t=1}^{T}$ ($T \geq 4$) by: (1)~DFT computation $\hat{\xi}_i(k) = \text{DFT}[\xi_i^{(t)}]$; (2)~retaining $K = 8$ spectral components; (3)~computing spectral energy $E_{\text{spec}} = \frac{1}{T}\sum_k |\hat{\xi}_i(k)|^2$; (4)~evaluating stability $\sigma_i = (1 + E_{\text{spec}})^{-1}$. A glyph forms only if $\sigma_i \geq 0.3$, ensuring bounded spectral energy~\cite{kelso1995dynamic}.
\end{definition}

\section{Reasoning Forge: Consensus-Based Multi-Agent Synchronization}
\label{sec:forge}

The Reasoning Forge~\cite{harrison2026codettefull} synchronizes heterogeneous cognitive agents through shared attractor dynamics rather than message-passing~\cite{wooldridge2009introduction}. This implements a mean-field consensus protocol~\cite{olfatisaber2007consensus,lasry2007mean}:
\begin{equation}
  A_{n+1}^{(i)} = f\!\left(A_n^{(i)},\; \frac{1}{N}\sum_{j=1}^{N} A_n^{(j)}\right) + \varepsilon_n^{(i)}
  \label{eq:forge_update}
\end{equation}
where the mean field $\frac{1}{N}\sum_j A_n^{(j)}$ acts as the shared attractor signal. Synchronization is achieved when all agents converge within tolerance $\delta < 0.1$~\cite{harrison2026codettefull}:
\begin{equation}
  \lim_{t \to \infty} |x_i(t) - x_j(t)| \to 0 \quad \forall\; i, j
  \label{eq:consensus}
\end{equation}

The Forge orchestrates eleven cognitive perspectives~\cite{harrison2026codettefull}: Newton (analytical), Da Vinci (creative), Human Intuition (empathetic), Neural Network (pattern recognition), Quantum Computing (probabilistic), Resilient Kindness (compassionate), Mathematical (quantitative), Philosophical (ethical), Copilot (collaborative), Bias Mitigation (fairness), and Psychological (behavioral).

\section{AEGIS Ethical Governance System}
\label{sec:aegis}

The AEGIS (Adaptive Ethical Governance and Immune System)~\cite{harrison2025aegisnexus,harrison2025ethics,harrison2026codettefull} functions as a reinforcement-aligned ethical regulator with recursive feedback:
\begin{equation}
  M(t) = \lambda \cdot R(t - \Delta t) + H(t) + \gamma \cdot \text{Learn}(t) + \mu \cdot \text{Regret}(t)
  \label{eq:ethical_anchor}
\end{equation}
where ethics evolves based on reward $R$, history $H$, learning signal $\gamma$, and regret feedback $\mu$. The regret term provides corrective feedback analogous to integral control~\cite{khalil2002nonlinear}. Simulation over 50 timesteps ($\lambda = 0.95$) demonstrates minimal ethical drift: $|\Delta M| = 0.012$, with mean $M(t) = 1.211 \pm 0.144$~\cite{harrison2026codettefull}.

AEGIS enforces: agent-specific logging with timestamped audit trails, ethical consideration tracking per reasoning chain, AES-256 encrypted thought storage, bias detection at the perspective-selection level, and kill-switch mechanisms for chains exceeding ethical thresholds~\cite{harrison2026codettefull,harrison2025ethics}.

\section{Memory Cocoons and Persistent Identity}
\label{sec:cocoons}

CognitionCocooner~\cite{harrison2025dreamcore,harrison2025dreamreal,harrison2026codettefull} encapsulates cognitive states as persistent ``cocoons''---encrypted snapshots including coherence, entanglement, resonance, and phase metrics. The cocoon stability criterion ensures bounded spectral energy~\cite{harrison2026codettefull}:
\begin{equation}
  \int_{-\infty}^{+\infty} |F(k)|^2 \, dk < \varepsilon_{\text{threshold}}
  \label{eq:cocoon_stability}
\end{equation}
where $F(k)$ is the Fourier transform of the cocoon's cognitive signal. Empirical validation with a three-component dream signal (40\,Hz gamma, 10\,Hz alpha, 4\,Hz theta) confirmed spectral energy of 76.57---well within the threshold of 100---yielding a 23.4\% stability margin~\cite{harrison2026codettefull}.

DreamReweaver~\cite{harrison2025dreamcore} synthesizes dormant cocoons into creative connections by reviving past analyses and generating novel combinations.

\section{Epistemic Metrics: Semantic-Space Instantiation}
\label{sec:epistemic}

The EpistemicMetrics module~\cite{harrison2026codettefull} provides a complementary semantic-space instantiation operating on natural-language agent outputs.

\textbf{Semantic Tension.} Given $N$ agent analyses $\{a_i\}$ with tf-vectors $\mathbf{v}_i$:
\begin{equation}
  \xi_{ij}^{\text{sem}} = 1 - \frac{\mathbf{v}_i \cdot \mathbf{v}_j}{\|\mathbf{v}_i\| \, \|\mathbf{v}_j\|}
  \label{eq:semantic_tension}
\end{equation}

\textbf{Ensemble Coherence.} With centroid $\mathbf{c} = \sum_i \mathbf{v}_i$:
\begin{equation}
  \Gamma^{\text{sem}} = \frac{1}{N} \sum_{i=1}^{N} \frac{\mathbf{v}_i \cdot \mathbf{c}}{\|\mathbf{v}_i\| \, \|\mathbf{c}\|}
  \label{eq:semantic_coherence}
\end{equation}

\textbf{Tension Productivity.} Measures whether divergent perspectives are \emph{integrated} rather than suppressed:
\begin{equation}
  P = \bar{\xi}^{\text{sem}} \cdot C_{\text{synth}}, \quad C_{\text{synth}} = \frac{1}{N}\sum_i \frac{|U_i \cap V_s|}{|U_i|}
  \label{eq:productivity}
\end{equation}
where $U_i$ is the set of terms unique to agent $i$ and $V_s$ is the synthesis vocabulary~\cite{harrison2026codettefull}. This metric is novel to the Codette framework.

\section{Adapter Training Infrastructure}
\label{sec:training}

\codette{} leverages LoRA~\cite{hu2021lora} and QLoRA~\cite{dettmers2023qlora} for parameter-efficient cognitive specialization on Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct~\cite{grattafiori2024llama}. Eight domain-specific adapters~\cite{harrison2025codettehf} are trained with rank 16, alpha 32, targeting \texttt{q\_proj}/\texttt{v\_proj} modules (13.6M / 4.55B trainable parameters, 0.3\%)~\cite{harrison2026codettefull}.

\subsection{GPU-Free CPU Training Pipelines}

Two novel GPU-free pipelines are contributed~\cite{harrison2026codettefull}:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Pipeline 1: CPU-Lean ($\sim$18\,GB RAM).} QLoRA 4-bit, rank~8, custom training loop bypassing \texttt{trl}/\texttt{SFTTrainer}, gradient checkpointing. Training: 8--24 hours per adapter on Intel Core Ultra 7 256V.
  \item \textbf{Pipeline 2: CPU-Offload ($\sim$8\,GB RAM).} Rank~4, SGD optimizer, 256-token sequence length, NVMe-backed page file (32\,GB). Enables 8-billion-parameter model fine-tuning on an 8\,GB laptop.
\end{itemize}
Both pipelines were validated on HP OmniBook 7 Flip 16 (Intel Core Ultra 7 256V, Intel Arc 140V 8\,GB, 16\,GB RAM)~\cite{harrison2026codettefull}.

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

\subsection{Strengths}
The \codette{} framework provides: (1)~a formally specified cognitive dynamical system with 9 definitions and a convergence theorem grounded in established mathematics~\cite{khalil2002nonlinear,strogatz2015nonlinear}; (2)~consensus-based synchronization with explicit connections to Kuramoto theory~\cite{kuramoto1984chemical} and multi-agent consensus~\cite{olfatisaber2007consensus}; (3)~architectural ethical governance rather than post-hoc filtering; (4)~GPU-free training enabling independent researcher access.

\subsection{Limitations}
The adapter pipeline targets Llama-3.1-8B~\cite{grattafiori2024llama}, smaller than frontier models. Context windows (4096--8192 tokens) constrain reasoning depth. Quantum-inspired operations are metaphorical. AEGIS governance reflects the developer's value system; critical applications require human oversight~\cite{harrison2026codettefull}.

\subsection{Future Work}
Directions include: formal verification of AEGIS via model checking, migration to larger base models, federated citizen-science deployment~\cite{harrison2025citizenscience}, and embodied RC+$\xi$ in robotic contexts~\cite{harrison2026codettefull}.

\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

This synthesis has presented the complete formal specification of the \codette{} cognitive architecture~\cite{harrison2026codettefull}, establishing precise mathematical relationships to dynamical systems theory~\cite{khalil2002nonlinear,strogatz2015nonlinear}, multi-agent consensus dynamics~\cite{olfatisaber2007consensus,ren2005consensus}, coupled oscillator synchronization~\cite{kuramoto1984chemical,acebron2005kuramoto}, and dynamical cognition~\cite{beer2000dynamical,kelso1995dynamic}. All constructs---RC+$\xi$, Reasoning Forge, AEGIS~\cite{harrison2025aegisnexus}, QuantumSpiderweb, Memory Cocoons~\cite{harrison2025dreamcore}---are formally defined, experimentally validated, and published under open-access licenses with permanent DOIs. The framework demonstrates that rigorous, formally grounded cognitive AI can be developed on consumer hardware, lowering barriers to independent AI research.
